Collection of astro projects. Some were completed in a day, some took several weeks. Some were completed for classes, most were for fun.

Light Curve of V* NR Lyr, an RR Lyrae star

Observational data was obtained from my TF. The top panel shows a portion of the field with some of the reference stars and the science star circled. The bottom panel is the light curve. The plotted points are the photometry data collected with our observations and the curve is Kepler data for the same star. The fit is remarkably close. Click here for a not-so-sciency overview of the (mostly coding) process and for a closer look at the figures.

M51

M67

Raw images were taken at Whipple Observatory in Arizona with a class, these are the reduced images after applying biases and flats. Notice that the image is divided into 4 different sections during the read out process from the CCD, each section has its own biases and flats. While the distiction may seem large in the final image, the pixel values don't vary by more than .001 between the regions. Click here to view them all. Click here to download the fits files.
