research projects

 

Degeneracy between Dark Matter and Baryonic Physics

This image shows the positions and relative sizes of dark matter haloes produced by a simulation. In this project, I explored the degeneracy between two dark matter models (CDM and sterile neutrino WDM) and two star formation efficiencies. With the upcoming launch of the JWST, the question arises of how much this groundbreaking new telescope will be able to tell us concerning the nature of dark matter. Contrary to previous works, we show that the JWST will likely not reveal a whole lot due to these degeneracies between DM models and baryonic physics. For more information, click here to see the full paper on the arxiv.

 

Stars Leave Home

Currently, I am studying the orbits of two different star clusters in the Perseus Cloud. These two clusters will be used to trace the kinematics of young stars leaving their respective star forming molecular clouds. The video on the right was made using glue software.

 

Light Curve of V* NR Lyr, an RR Lyrae star

Observational data was obtained from my TF. The top panel shows a portion of the field with some of the reference stars and the science star circled. The bottom panel is the light curve. The plotted points are the photometry data collected with our observations and the curve is Kepler data for the same star. The fit is remarkably close.
